How It Works

This generator is based on the common song-form descriptions in Song structure on Wikipedia.

In this app, the selected form controls the order and role of each section, while the generated music is produced with a simple rule-based sequence engine.

AABA uses two A sections, a contrasting B section, and a final A return. ABAB alternates A and B material. ABABCB extends that idea with a bridge-like C section before the last B return. Verse-Chorus uses intro, verse, pre-chorus, chorus, bridge, and outro roles so the sections feel more distinct.

Each part gets its own profile for density, velocity range, octave placement, and melodic contour. Notes are still randomized inside the selected scale and root, but the randomness is shaped by the section type, so choruses tend to be denser and higher, bridges contrast more, and intros/outros stay lighter.

Clock modes affect timing and MIDI transport: Clock off runs the internal sequencer only; Clock out sends MIDI clock and transport messages; Clock in arms playback and waits for an external MIDI Start (or incoming clock ticks) before the song begins.

In short, structure controls the section order, profile and contrast; scale/root constrain the pitch pool; density adjusts how active each part feels; and randomness selects the exact notes, octaves, velocities, and rests.
